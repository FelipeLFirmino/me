## My Take

I found this book pretty interesting. Even though it says obvious things, those things *need* to be said — because most people forget them when developing a product. Developers often aim so much for a public that's already used to software and the internet that they forget the people who aren't, which is the majority of the world's population.

---

## 1. Defining Usability

Usability is defined by three fundamental pillars that allow a person with normal (or below-average) knowledge to use a system:

- **Learnability:** The ability to discover how to use the interface.
- **Effectiveness:** The ability to complete the desired task.
- **Efficiency:** Completing the task without the effort required being greater than the benefit gained.
- **Additional factors:** Pleasantness and memorability (especially relevant in mobile contexts).

---

## 2. Navigation and Home Page Structure

Navigation should be **brief, intuitive (clear), and timely**. When done well, it eliminates all mystery for the user.

**Questions the Home Page must answer:**

When landing on a site, the user should immediately identify:

- What is this?
- What can I do here?
- What do they have?
- Why should I stay here and not go somewhere else?

**Interface Elements:**

- **Visual Hierarchy:** Use a clear hierarchy and follow design conventions.
- **Breadcrumbs:** Should be placed at the top, using the ">" separator between levels, with the last item in bold.
- **Page Identification:** The user must always know the site ID, the current page name, the main sections, and the local navigation options.
- **Search:** There must be a clear field for performing searches.

## 3. Research and Testing Methods

**Focus Groups**
- Audience: 5 to 10 people.
- Goal: Quickly gather opinions, feelings, and reactions to ideas or products.

**Usability Tests**
- Audience: One person at a time.
- Goal: Observe the user trying to perform common tasks to detect points of confusion or frustration.
- Note: Don't rely solely on your own judgment — test with people outside the organization to identify blind spots.

---

## 4. Usability Test Script (60 minutes)

| Step | Duration | Description |
| :--- | :------- | :---------- |
| Welcome | 4 min | Explain how the test will work to align expectations. |
| Questions | 2 min | Personal questions to relax the participant and understand their technical level. |
| Homepage Tour | 3 min | The participant looks at the Home and says what they understand from it. |
| The Tasks | 35 min | The core of the test. The participant performs tasks thinking out loud. Rule: Do not give hints or influence the user. |
| Probing | 5 min | Questions about what happened during the test. |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Thank you, payment, and closing. |

---

## 5. Practical Recommendations

- **Company Mission:** Do not use the corporate mission statement as a welcome text on the Home Page. Long, institutional texts are usually ignored by users.
- **Test Intervention:** If the participant asks for help, ask: *"What would you do if I weren't here?"* Only help if they are completely stuck or frustrated.
